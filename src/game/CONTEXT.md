# Game Engine

A game engine powering a worker-placement idle RPG with a simulated trade market as its core resource loop. Characters explore the world autonomously while the player manages their team and trades resources to fund expansion.

## Language

### Player & Team

**Player**:
The human controlling the game. Manages the market, assembles the team, and assigns characters to expeditions.
_Avoid_: User, trader, merchant

**Character**:
A team member with a character sheet (stats, skills, advantages/disadvantages) inspired by GURPS and Fragged Kingdom 2. Each character is distinct and not interchangeable.
_Avoid_: Unit, worker, agent, hero

**Character Sheet**:
The full definition of a character — attributes, skills, advantages, and disadvantages. Determines which expeditions a character can attempt and how effectively.
_Avoid_: Stats, profile, loadout

**Skill**:
A character capability that can be developed over time. Drawn from GURPS-style point-buy; no fixed classes. Characters may equip a limited active skill set at a time (Guild Wars 1-style skill bar).
_Avoid_: Ability, power, talent

**Team**:
The player's current roster of characters. Starts empty; grown through play.
_Avoid_: Party, guild, crew

### Exploration

**Expedition**:
An implied, timer-based task a character is assigned to. The character acts autonomously and returns with resources or outcomes when the timer expires. Inspired by Arcanum: Theory of Magic.
_Avoid_: Quest, mission, job, run

**Assignment**:
The act of sending a character on an expedition. A form of worker placement — each character can only be assigned to one expedition at a time.
_Avoid_: Deploy, dispatch, send

**Resource**:
Any item, material, or currency a character can bring back from an expedition. Feeds into the market.
_Avoid_: Loot, drop, reward

### Market

**Market**:
The simulated trade system at the core of the game loop. Prices move dynamically based on supply and demand. The player interacts with it to convert resources into capital.
_Avoid_: Shop, store, economy

**Orderbook**:
The internal representation of the market — a live list of buy and sell orders at various prices. Drives price discovery.
_Avoid_: Price list, catalog

**Order**:
A buy or sell intent placed by the player (or the simulation) into the orderbook. May be filled immediately or sit until matched.
_Avoid_: Trade, transaction, purchase

**Capital**:
The player's accumulated wealth, generated by selling resources through the market. Used to grow the team and unlock new expeditions.
_Avoid_: Money, gold, currency, funds

**Price**:
The current market value of a resource, determined by the balance of supply and demand in the orderbook.
_Avoid_: Cost, value, rate

## Example dialogue

> **Dev**: "When the player sends a character to gather iron, is that an expedition?"
>
> **Designer**: "Yes — you assign the character, the timer runs, iron comes back."
>
> **Dev**: "And the iron goes straight to the player's inventory, or does it hit the market?"
>
> **Designer**: "Player's inventory first. They decide when to sell it by placing an order."
>
> **Dev**: "So the player places a sell order, and the market simulation matches it against demand?"
>
> **Designer**: "Exactly. That match sets the price. If everyone's selling iron, the price drops."
